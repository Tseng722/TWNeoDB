About TWNeoDB

Tumor neoantigens are known for their high immunogenicity. There are two main types of neoantigens that have shown potential for being shared among patients. The first type is mutated tumor-specific antigens (mTSAs), which originate from somatic mutations in tumor cells. The second type is aberrantly expressed tumor-specific antigens (aeTSAs), which arise due to epigenetic modifications or abnormal RNA splicing events. These tumor neoantigens can associate with major histocompatibility complex (MHC) molecules and be recognized by T cell receptors (TCRs), leading to the activation of the immune system to target and destroy cancer cells. Although several neoantigen databases have been developed, they predominantly focus on the Western population and largely consist of peptides from mTSAs. In contrast, our database includes peptides from both mTSAs and aeTSAs, with a particular focus on the Taiwanese population.
